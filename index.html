How Age Is Calculated

Age calculation sounds simple — subtract your birth year from the current year — but getting it exactly right takes more than that. Our calculator works from the actual calendar date, not just the year: it counts full years elapsed, then the remaining months, then the remaining days, adjusting automatically for months of different lengths (28 to 31 days) and leap years (which add a day roughly every four years).

That's why two people born in the same year can have noticeably different "exact ages" on the same day — someone born in January has already had their birthday by mid-year, while someone born in November hasn't. Calendar-year age (just subtracting birth year from current year) can be off by a full year compared to your true, birthday-adjusted age.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate exact age?

To calculate exact age, subtract your birth year from the current year, then adjust for months and days. Our age calculator does this automatically, giving you precise age in years, months, days, hours, and minutes.

What is my age if I was born in 1990?

If you were born in 1990, your age depends on the current year and your birth month/day. Use our age calculator to enter your exact birth date and get your precise age in years, months, and days.

How many days until my next birthday?

Enter your birth date into our calculator, and it will automatically calculate exactly how many days remain until your next birthday. It also shows your next birthday date.

Is this age calculator accurate?

Yes, our age calculator is highly accurate. It accounts for leap years and different month lengths to give you the exact number of years, months, days, hours, and minutes between your birth date and today.

Why is my "exact age" different from just subtracting birth year from this year?

Simple year subtraction ignores where your birthday falls in the calendar. If your birthday hasn't happened yet this year, that method overstates your age by one year. Our calculator checks the actual month and day, not just the year, so the result is always your true current age.

Can I calculate someone else's age, not just my own?

Yes — the calculator works with any birth date, not just yours. It's commonly used to check a child's age for school enrollment cutoffs, verify eligibility for age-restricted programs, or simply settle a birthday debate.
